\begin{abstract}
This extended abstract is a compressed paper in preparation extending the work of \cite{cliff2023} to exploit the topological construction of multivariate time series as graphs.

Graph neural networks for multivariate time series require a relational graph that is rarely given. We parameterise graph construction over a vocabulary of $K \approx 125$ named pairwise statistics --- causal, spectral, linear, information-theoretic --- computed via \texttt{pyspi} \citep{cliff2023}, a package created by our group for massive feature extraction in multivariate time series. A learned weight vector $\mathbf{w}$, jointly optimised with a message-passing neural network, selects which statistical notions of coupling are task-relevant and produces an interpretable statistical signature of the relational structure. We show that symmetric construction operators cannot distinguish Markov-equivalent topologies, and confirm this empirically: symmetric baselines remain at or below 59\% on a synthetic classification task while vocabulary-based models achieve near-perfect accuracy. The learned $\mathbf{w}$ recovers spectral Granger causality (SGC) as the dominant coupling mode, matching the generating process.
\end{abstract}

%-----------------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
%-----------------------------------------------------------------------

Message-passing neural networks (MPNNs) propagate information along relational structure \citep{gilmer2017,battaglia2018}. In multivariate time series (MTS) settings --- neuroscience, climate, finance --- this structure is unobserved and must be constructed from data.

Current practice follows two regimes. The first fixes the graph from a single statistic: Pearson correlation for functional connectivity \citep{bullmore2009}, spatial distance for traffic \citep{li2018dcrnn}, or transfer entropy for causal graphs \citep{duan2023}. This commits to one mathematical notion of dependence before learning begins. \citet{liu2025} benchmarked 239 pairwise statistics on fMRI data and found substantial variation across methods, confirming that this choice is consequential. \citet{qi2025} showed that message-passing consistently degrades performance on functional connectivity graphs when the construction statistic is naively chosen, establishing graph construction --- not architecture --- as the critical design variable.

The second regime learns the graph from latent embeddings \citep{kipf2018,wu2019,wu2020}. This recovers flexibility at the cost of semantics, i.e., a learned edge weight carries no information about what form of dependence it represents. In scientific applications where the graph itself is the object of interest, this opacity is a fundamental limitation.

We propose a third approach which interpolates between the fixed-statistic and latent regimes. For each edge $e_{ij}$ between nodes of an MTS, we precompute a $K$-dimensional descriptor $\mathbf{E}_{ij}$ whose coordinates are semantically interpretable pairwise statistics --- transfer entropy, spectral Granger causality, coherence, covariance, and approximately 125 others --- drawn from six mathematical families using \texttt{pyspi} \citep{cliff2023}. Graph construction becomes a linear probe of this descriptor space:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:adj}
A_{ij} = \mathrm{softplus}\left(b + \mathbf{w}^\top \mathbf{E}_{ij}\right), 
\end{equation}
with top-$d$ sparisification per node, where $\mathbf{w} \in \mathbb{R}^K$ is jointly optimised with the downstream MPNN \citep[cf.][]{alain2017}. Group lasso regularisation \citep{yuan2006} over SPI families encourages family-level selection, producing an interpretable statistical signature of the task's relational structure.

%-----------------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Symmetric Construction Cannot Distinguish Markov-Equivalent Topologies}
\label{sec:theory}
%-----------------------------------------------------------------------

The choice of construction statistic constrains what an MPNN can learn. Consider three 3-node VAR(1) motifs: chain ($A \to B \to C$), fork ($A \leftarrow B \to C$), and collider ($A \to B \leftarrow C$). All three share skeleton $A$--$B$--$C$. Chain and fork are Markov-equivalent \citep{verma1990}, i.e., they encode identical conditional independence relations, so any symmetric feature $f(X^{(i)}, X^{(j)}) = f(X^{(j)}, X^{(i)})$ assigns identical edge weights to both, regardless of sample size. No MPNN operating on such a graph can exceed $2/3$ accuracy on the three-class problem.

Directed statistics break this equivalence by considering spectral Granger causality (SGC), which measures the causal influence of one time series to another. In the chain, $\mathrm{SGC}(A \to B) \gg \mathrm{SGC}(B \to A)$; in the fork, the asymmetry pattern differs. The asymmetric descriptor $\mathbf{E}_{ij} \neq \mathbf{E}_{ji}$ from directed statistics in the vocabulary provides the information needed for discrimination.

%-----------------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}
%-----------------------------------------------------------------------

Given an $M$-chanelled multivariate time series $X_t^{(i)} \in \mathbb{R}^{M \times T}$, we compute $K$ pairwise statistics per ordered pair of channels via \texttt{pyspi} \citep{cliff2023}, yielding $\mathbf{E} \in \mathbb{R}^{M \times M \times K}$, spanning six families: \textbf{causal} (transfer entropy, Granger causality, spectral GC --- asymmetric), \textbf{spectral} (coherence, PLV --- mixed), \textbf{linear} (covariance, precision --- symmetric), \textbf{information-theoretic} (mutual information), \textbf{distance} (DTW, Euclidean), and \textbf{rank} (Spearman, Kendall). The causal family provides the asymmetry that symmetric operators lack.

The adjacency $A_{ij}$ (Eq.~\ref{eq:adj}) is sparsified to the top-$d$ outgoing edges per node. Retained edges carry the full descriptor $\mathbf{E}_{ij}$ as attributes. An edge network $\phi(\mathbf{E}_{ij}) = \mathrm{MLP}(\mathbf{E}_{ij})$ conditions messages on this descriptor:

\begin{footnotesize}
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{m}_{ij} = \mathrm{MLP}\ \left(\mathbf{h}_j \| \mathbf{h}_i \| \phi(\mathbf{E}_{ij})\right), \;\; \mathbf{h}_i' = \mathbf{h}_i + \mathrm{LN}\!\left(\textstyle\sum_j A_{ij}\,\mathbf{m}_{ij}\right)
\end{equation}
\end{footnotesize}

The vocabulary informs both topology (via $\mathbf{w}$) and message content (via $\phi$). Global pooling and a classifier produce graph-level predictions. Training minimises the task loss and is regularised by sparsity penalties on $\mathbf{w}$ and a group lasso penalty to emphasise family-level selection:
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:training_eq}
\mathcal{L}(\theta)=\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{task}} + \lambda_1 \|\mathbf{w} \|_1 + \lambda_g \sum_{g\in \mathcal{G}}{\|\mathbf{w}_g\|_2}.
\end{equation}

We use the Adam optimiser with LR warmup (60 epochs) and restart selection (best of 2 initialisations).

\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}
%-----------------------------------------------------------------------

We generate $M{=}10$ node VAR(1) processes ($T{=}500$) with a 3-node directed motif (chain, fork, or collider) embedded among 7 nuisance AR(1) channels. This directly tests the theory of Section~\ref{sec:theory}: chain and fork are Markov-equivalent, so symmetric operators are provably limited. Coupling strengths $\alpha \sim \mathrm{Uniform}(0.3, 0.7)$; 1500 instances per class; $K{=}125$ statistics after variance filtering; 30 seeds.

\textbf{The SPI vocabulary is the dominant inductive bias.} All vocabulary-consuming models dramatically outperform non-SPI baselines: at $n{=}500$, SPI models achieve 99--100\% while Correlation plateaus at 59\% and Latent remains at chance (31\%). The oracle-best single statistic (spectral Granger causality) achieves near-perfect peak accuracy but exhibits catastrophic seed failures --- 2 of 30 seeds collapse below 40\% F1 at intermediate sample sizes. The full vocabulary eliminates these failures entirely (std $\leq 2$\% at $n \geq 50$), providing robustness through the diversity of 125 complementary estimators.

\textbf{Symmetric construction is limited, as predicted.} Correlation never exceeds 59\%, well below the $2/3$ theoretical ceiling, because top-$d$ sparsification discards motif edges in favour of high-correlation nuisance pairs. The latent model performs at chance across all 30 seeds and sample sizes, confirming that symmetric node-embedding construction with no statistical prior cannot access the chain--fork distinction.

\textbf{The learned $\mathbf{w}$ is an interpretable statistical signature.} Under group lasso, the causal family (48 SPIs) carries $6\times$ the $L_2$ norm of the next family. The five highest-weighted individual SPIs are all directed temporal measures: spectral Granger causality in the 0.25--0.5\,Hz band and time-domain Granger causality variants. For a VAR(1) process, causal structure manifests as asymmetric spectral transfer functions --- the model recovers this from data.

An edge-ablation study (same adjacency, zeroed edge features) drops performance to 93$\pm$11\% at $n{=}500$, confirming that the MPNN actively uses the SPI descriptors to condition messages, not merely the learned topology. Full ablation results are in the Appendix.

%-----------------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}
%-----------------------------------------------------------------------

Graph structure learning methods universally use latent embeddings \citep{zhu2021}, sacrificing interpretability. CauGNN \citep{duan2023} constructs graphs from a single statistic; ADLGNN \citep{sriramulu2023} initialises from statistics but overwrites them with attention. Our approach retains a vocabulary of named statistics with learnable weights that remain inspectable after training.

The statistical signature produced by $\mathbf{w}$ --- identifying spectral Granger causality as the dominant coupling mode --- constitutes a testable hypothesis about the data-generating process. This is a form of output that latent graph learners cannot produce. On real-world MTS (EEG, fMRI, climate), the same mechanism would identify which notion of coupling drives the task, yielding domain-interpretable graph structure directly from learned parameters. The current study validates the framework on synthetic data; real-world experiments are underway.
